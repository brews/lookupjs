Sacramento & Colorado River>> semi-permanent western US high

This version of lookup focuses on patterns between the Sacramento River ("Sacramento"), the Colorado River ("LeesFerry"), the wintertime latitude of the seasonal Rocky High ("HighLat"), and the relative intensity of the wintertime Rocky High ("HighValue").

The data is described in detail below.

"Sacramento" is a water year time series of unimpaired runoff, or full natural flow, for the Sacramento River. The data has been transformed into rank-percentiles. The series is the sum of natural flow from four river gauges: Sacramento River at Bend Bridge, Feather River at Oroville, Yuba River near Smartville, and American River at Folsom Lake. This index is also known as the "Four River Index" or "Four Basin Index".

"LeesFerry" is a water year time series of natural flow for the Colorado River. The data has been transformed into rank-percentiles. The data is calculated from the Lee's Ferry river gauge. This gauge measures the Colorado River streamflow from the Upper Colorado River basin. Source.

"HighLat" is latitude of the center for the semi-permanent high-pressure atmosphere circulation feature over the North American Cordillera from November through April at 500 mb geopoential height. This value is estimated from a method described in Malevich and Woodhouse 2014 using NCEP/NCAR reanalysis data.

"HighValue" is the estimated intensity or magnitude (in units m geopotential height) of the center for the semi-permanent high-pressure atmosphere circulation feature over the North American Cordillera from November through April at 500 mb geopoential height. The value is an anomaly from the 500 mb geopotential height field with the zonal mean removed from the data. This value is estimated from a method described in Malevich and Woodhouse 2014 using NCEP/NCAR reanalysis data.
